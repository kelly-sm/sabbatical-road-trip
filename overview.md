## Standout Camping Spots (Anchor Points)

These are the must-target overnight stops — plan reservations around these first.

- **Gold Bluffs Beach, Prairie Creek Redwoods SP** — Black sand beach camping with elk, steps from Fern Canyon. One of the best van spots on the trip.
- **Kirk Creek Campground, Big Sur** — Bluff sites directly above the Pacific. Books out months in advance — **reserve ASAP** on recreation.gov.
- **Samuel P. Taylor State Park (Marin)** — Old-growth redwood forest camp, 45 min from SF. Point Reyes NS campgrounds are walk-in only — not accessible by van.
- **Dead Horse Point State Park, Utah** — Overlook camping 2,000 feet above the Colorado River. Reserve on utahstateparks.reserveamerica.com.
- **BLM dispersed land around Moab** — Free, legal, red rock views. No reservation needed; scout spots via Campendium or iOverlander.

## April Conditions & Watch-Outs

- **Big Sur:** Some areas have had storm-related closures. Kirk Creek (south end) is typically reliable. Confirm Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park status closer to date.
- **Bryce Canyon:** Sits at 8,000+ ft — freezing nights possible in early April. Pack accordingly.
- **Zion:** Shuttle required to access the canyon floor from mid-spring onward. Angels Landing requires a permit lottery (even for the walk to Scout Lookout). Check nps.gov/zion for timed entry requirements.
- **Grand Canyon South Rim:** Open year-round. April is ideal — not yet summer hot, not winter cold.
- **California Coast:** Expect morning fog, especially around SF and Monterey. Spring wildflowers can be spectacular.
