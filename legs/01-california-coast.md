# Leg 1: California Coast
**Seattle → Oregon Coast → Redwoods → San Francisco → Monterey → Big Sur → Los Angeles**
**Dates:** April 6–16 (10 nights, 11 days)

## ⚠️ Current Conditions & Watch-Outs (as of March 2026)

## Celiac Dining Strategy for This Leg

This route passes through some of the best Celiac-safe dining in the country. A few principles:

- Always say **"Celiac disease"**, not "gluten-free" — it signals medical necessity and changes how kitchens respond.
- **DEDICATED** = 100% gluten-free facility. Safest. **CELIAC-AWARE** = shared kitchen but with dedicated fryers, separate prep, trained staff, and strong celiac community reviews. **GF-FRIENDLY** = menu options only, not vetted for cross-contamination.
- **Stock the van** before entering Big Sur (no reliable options) and before Joshua Tree.
- **Natural co-ops** are reliable fallbacks: Wildberries Marketplace (Arcata), New Leaf Community Market (Santa Cruz), Erewhon (multiple LA locations).

## Day-by-Day

### Day 1 — Apr 6: Seattle → Oregon Coast
**Drive:** ~3.5 hrs to Cannon Beach (175 miles via US-101 or US-26 west then south)

**Stops:**
- **Cannon Beach** — Haystack Rock at low tide, walkable beach town, good spot for lunch or coffee. Check tide charts.
- **Tillamook** — Tillamook Creamery if you want a cheese detour (worth it). ~45 min south of Cannon Beach. **Celiac note:** Ice cream and cheese are safe; skip the bakery café items.

**Celiac dining — Cannon Beach:**
- **Public Coast Brewing Co.** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — 264 3rd St. **This is the standout celiac stop on the Oregon Coast.** The owner's son has Celiac disease, which shapes the whole kitchen culture. Dedicated fryer, gloves changed on request, separate containers. Almost the entire menu available GF: fish and chips, onion rings, burgers with GF buns, clam chowder. Listed in the National Celiac Association restaurant directory. Worth arriving in Cannon Beach hungry.
- **Cape Lookout State Park** or **Beverly Beach** for the night if pushing further south

**Camp:**
- **Cape Lookout State Park** (Tillamook County) — forested sites near the beach, reservecalifornia equivalent: [oregonstateparks.org](https://oregonstateparks.org). Good first night.
- Or push to **Beverly Beach State Park** (Newport, OR) — 128 sites, ocean side, 5 min from the beach.

**Distance from Cannon Beach to Beverly Beach:** ~1.5 hrs. Both are solid.

### Day 2 — Apr 7: Oregon Coast → Redwoods
**Drive:** Beverly Beach → Crescent City, CA (~5.5 hrs, 320 miles via US-101)

**Stops along US-101:**
- **Sea Lion Caves** (Florence, OR) — largest sea cave in North America. Worth a stop if timing works.
- **Oregon Dunes NRA** — dramatic coastal dunes near Reedsport, good for a walk-stretch
- **Trees of Mystery** (Klamath, CA) — kitschy but fun roadside stop as you enter Redwood country

**Celiac dining en route:**
- **Lunch:** Pack from the van — options are thin through coastal Oregon. **Wildberries Marketplace** (Arcata co-op, ~halfway) has a deli with labeled GF items for a restock.
- **Dinner in Eureka:** **Humboldt Bay Bistro** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — 1436 2nd St, Eureka, (707) 443-7339. Bay-front French-Californian. Their menu explicitly differentiates celiac-safe dishes from general GF items — unusually transparent. Celiac-trained staff. The best dinner option between the Oregon border and Bodega Bay, and worth timing your arrival for. Reserve ahead.

**Camp:** Arrive at Redwoods by late afternoon. Two great options:
- **Gold Bluffs Beach Campground (Prairie Creek Redwoods SP)** ⭐ — 26 sites on a black sand beach. Elk wander through camp. Steps from Fern Canyon trailhead. Reserve on [reservecalifornia.com](https://www.reservecalifornia.com). No hookups needed (you're self-contained). Arrive before dark — Davison Road is unpaved and narrow.
- **Elk Prairie Campground** — Alternative if Gold Bluffs is full. Surrounded by old-growth redwoods, elk frequent the meadow at dawn and dusk.

**Reservation:** Book Gold Bluffs Beach ASAP — 🔴 reserve on [reservecalifornia.com](https://www.reservecalifornia.com)

---

### Day 3 — Apr 8: Full Day in Redwoods
**Stay:** Gold Bluffs Beach (2nd night) or move to Elk Prairie

**Best things to do:**
- **Fern Canyon** — 30-min loop through a slot canyon with 50-ft fern-covered walls. One of the most unique hikes in California. Featured in Jurassic Park 2. Easy/flat, ~1 mile.
- **Prairie Creek Redwoods** — drive or walk among the giants. The Revelation Trail is accessible and stunning.
- **Newton B. Drury Scenic Parkway** — a 10-mile drive through old-growth groves, no trucks allowed. Pull-offs for short walks.
- **Elk viewing** — Roosevelt elk herds are common near Elk Prairie, especially morning and evening.
- Optional: **Avenue of the Giants** (~1.5 hrs south near Humboldt Redwoods SP) — best of the giant sequoia grove drives. Could do this en route to SF the next day.

### Day 4 — Apr 9: Redwoods → San Francisco / Marin
**Drive:** Crescent City → Samuel P. Taylor SP (~5 hrs, 325 miles via US-101)

**Stops:**
- **Avenue of the Giants** (Humboldt) — if not done yesterday, factor in 1 hr detour off US-101. Drive the 31-mile scenic route through Humboldt Redwoods SP.
- **Humboldt Bay** (Eureka/Arcata) — short waterfront stop for coffee or lunch
- **Bodega Bay** — charming coastal town ~1.5 hrs north of the Golden Gate. Good lunch stop. **Spud Point Crab Company** ✅ — outdoor crab stand, fresh Dungeness crab by the pound, naturally GF. Cash or card, picnic tables on the dock. Outstanding.
- **Marin detour on the way in: Flour Craft Bakery** ✅ DEDICATED — Two Marin locations (Mill Valley / San Anselmo). 100% GF facility, all supplier flours certified. Artisan pastries, breads, savory toasts, quiche, salads. Great place to pick up snacks and breakfast items to stock the van. Featured by the Celiac Community Foundation of Northern California.

**Camp: Samuel P. Taylor State Park** ⭐ — 61 sites in a dense old-growth coastal redwood forest along Papermill Creek. Showers, flush toilets. 15 miles west of San Rafael on Sir Francis Drake Blvd, ~45 min from Golden Gate. Great Marin base camp.
- Reserve on [reservecalifornia.com](https://www.reservecalifornia.com) — opens 3 months in advance (book in early January)

### Day 5 — Apr 10: San Francisco + Marin
**Stay:** Samuel P. Taylor (2nd night)

**Best things to do:**
- **Marin Headlands** — drive up to Hawk Hill for the best Golden Gate Bridge views anywhere. Zero crowds compared to the bridge overlooks. The Coastal Trail here is excellent.
- **Muir Woods** — old-growth coast redwood grove, 30 min from camp. **Requires timed-entry permit** — reserve at [gomuirwoods.com](https://www.gomuirwoods.com) weeks in advance.
- **Sausalito** — waterfront town just north of the Golden Gate. Great for lunch, walk the boardwalk.
- **San Francisco day trip** — take the ferry from Sausalito into the Ferry Building for the best approach to the city. Walk the Embarcadero, explore the Mission if time allows.
- ⚠️ **Skip: Tartine Bakery** (Mission) — iconic SF sourdough bakery, no safe Celiac options.

**Celiac dining highlights — this is one of the best days on the entire trip for GF eating:**

- **Copita Tequileria y Comida** ⭐ DEDICATED — 739 Bridgeway, Sausalito. **100% gluten-free kitchen** — no wheat enters the building. Upscale waterfront Mexican by chef Joanne Weir. Fresh tortillas, tacos, enchiladas, mole. Lunch here before or after Muir Woods is the move — order anything on the menu freely.

- **Mariposa Baking Co.** ⭐ DEDICATED — Ferry Building, Suite 32. 100% GF artisan bakery. Breads, croissants, cinnamon rolls, bagels, sandwiches — eat freely while your non-GF partner shops the market. Hours: Mon–Fri 9:30–4, Sat 9–4, Sun 10–4.

- **Pica Pica Arepa Kitchen** ⭐ DEDICATED — 401 Valencia St, Mission District (SF). **One of the most rigorously Celiac-safe restaurants on the route.** 100% GF Venezuelan: arepas, cachapas, yuca fries. They test recipes with NIMA sensors and post results publicly. Official partner of the Celiac Community Foundation of Northern California. If you're heading into the city, plan your Mission/Dolores neighborhood walk around lunch here.

- **Hog Island Oyster Co.** ✅ — Marshall, 20 min from camp on Tomales Bay. Raw and BBQ oysters are naturally GF. One of the best experiences on the whole coast — oyster farm with a direct retail bar on Tomales Bay. Reserve a table.

- **Saltwater Oyster Depot** ✅ GF-friendly — Inverness, near camp. Outstanding local oysters and seafood. Confirm sauces when ordering.

### Day 6 — Apr 11: Drive to Monterey
**Drive:** Samuel P. Taylor → Monterey (~2.5 hrs, 115 miles via US-101 or Hwy 1 through Half Moon Bay)

**Recommended route: Take Hwy 1 through Half Moon Bay** — adds ~45 min but is beautiful. Stop at:
- **Half Moon Bay** — charming coastal town, good coffee stop. Celiac dining is thin here — best to eat in SF before leaving or wait for Santa Cruz. If you need a meal: **Sam's Chowder House** has some GF options (call ahead; their chowder base can be done GF on request but it's a shared kitchen).
- **Año Nuevo State Park** — elephant seal colony (seasonal, some seals present in April)
- **Santa Cruz** — worth building time around **Switch Bakery** (see below). Also: West Cliff Drive walk, boardwalk area.

**Celiac dining — Santa Cruz:**
- **Switch Bakery** ⭐ DEDICATED — downtown Santa Cruz. **Don't skip this.** 100% GF, soy-free, corn-free full bistro: flatbread pizzas, sandwiches, salads, baguettes, focaccia, eclairs, cream puffs, cookies, pies. Evening dessert lounge. A Celiac diner can walk in and order anything — breads, pastries, savory meals. The kind of place that barely exists. Build lunch or dessert around it. Check hours before arriving.

**Camp near Monterey:**
- **Veterans Memorial Park** (Monterey city) — 40 sites, some with ocean views, 10 min walk to Cannery Row. Convenient but urban. Reserve at monterey.org.
- **Laguna Seca Recreation Area** (~10 miles east of Monterey) — more spacious, less scenic but well-equipped

**Evening:** Cannery Row waterfront walk, sunset over the bay.

**Celiac dining — Monterey/Carmel:**
- **Nece's GF Bakery** ⭐ DEDICATED — 25 Soledad Dr, Monterey, (831) 316-9768. The only dedicated GF bakery in Monterey County. No outside food allowed inside (protects celiac customers from cross-contamination). Cinnamon rolls, muffins, morning buns, sandwiches. **Stock up here before driving into Big Sur** — there are no reliable Celiac-safe options once you leave Monterey.
- **Carmel's Bistro Giovanni** ⭐ CELIAC-AWARE — San Carlos between 5th & 6th, Carmel. Best Celiac dinner in the Monterey/Carmel area. Every staff member trained on Celiac protocols. Separate cookware and prep area. GF pasta, GF bread, GF dessert. Celiac community positive reviews including from diners using gluten-detection devices. Reserve ahead.
- **Passionfish** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — Pacific Grove. Widely regarded as the best overall restaurant in the area. Known for accommodating all dietary restrictions; speak to them directly about Celiac when reserving.

### Day 7 — Apr 12: Monterey → Big Sur
**Drive:** Monterey → Kirk Creek Campground (~1.5 hrs, 50 miles via Hwy 1 south) — stop at everything along the way

**Stops (all on Hwy 1 heading south):**
- **17-Mile Drive** (Pebble Beach) — private toll road (~$12), stunning Cypress Point and Lone Cypress. Enter from the Pacific Grove gate. Allow 1–1.5 hrs.
- **Carmel-by-the-Sea** — quaint village, worth a short walk. If you didn't eat at Carmel's Bistro Giovanni the night before, this is a good lunch stop. Grab coffee at Carmel Valley Coffee Roasting Co.
- **Point Sur Lighthouse** — dramatic rock island lighthouse. Tours available weekends.
- **Bixby Creek Bridge** — iconic Big Sur shot, best photo stop on the whole coast. Pull off on the north side.
- **McWay Falls** (Julia Pfeiffer Burns SP) — 80-ft waterfall dropping directly onto the beach. 10-min walk from parking. One of the iconic images of Big Sur.
- ⚠️ **Rocky Creek Bridge** — one lane, expect a 10–15 min delay around mile marker ~66

**Camp: Kirk Creek Campground** ⭐⭐ — 33 sites on a bluff directly above the Pacific Ocean. Wind can be strong. No water on site (your van tank is perfect for this). Best sites: **9, 11, 15, 17, 19, 21** — request these specifically, they're right over the water.
- 🔴 **Reserve IMMEDIATELY** on [recreation.gov](https://www.recreation.gov) — books 6 months out. Check for cancellations and set alerts.

### Day 8 — Apr 13: Full Day Big Sur
**Stay:** Kirk Creek (2nd night) or move to Plaskett Creek (adjacent, more sheltered)

**Best things to do:**
- **Andrew Molera State Park** — beach walk (2.5 miles round trip to the beach), great birding in April. Easy, flat, stunning.
- **Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park** — check current access (has had storm closures). Pfeiffer Falls trail if open (1.6 miles round trip, lush canyon waterfall).
- **Ewoldsen Trail** (Julia Pfeiffer Burns SP) — best moderate hike in Big Sur. ~4.5 miles, canyon to ridge with ocean views. Strenuous in places but manageable.
- **Plaskett Rock / Sand Dollar Beach** — beautiful beach 2 miles south of Kirk Creek. One of the few easily accessible Big Sur beaches.
- **Sunset at your campsite** — Kirk Creek bluff sites have one of the best sunset views on the California coast.

**Celiac dining — Big Sur:**
- ⚠️ **No reliably Celiac-safe restaurant options in Big Sur.** Stock the van at Nece's in Monterey before arriving. Van cooking is the plan for most meals here.
- **Nepenthe** — worth a sunset cocktail and the view. The bar is safe; their menu has some naturally GF items (call ahead about kitchen practices if you want a full meal). The experience is worth the stop even if you just have drinks.
- ⚠️ **Big Sur Bakery** — skip for Celiac. Bakery environment with high cross-contamination risk.

### Day 9 — Apr 14: Big Sur → Los Angeles
**Drive:** Kirk Creek → Los Angeles (~5 hrs, 270 miles via Hwy 1 to US-101)

**Stops:**
- **Hearst Castle** (San Simeon) — if touring appeals (~2 hrs). Book tours in advance at hearstcastle.org.
- **Morro Bay** — distinctive volcanic rock in the harbor, great stretch stop. **Luna Red** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE (SLO, 20 min inland — worth a detour for dinner; global small plates, consistently Celiac-praised). For a quicker Morro Bay stop: fresh seafood at the docks is naturally GF.
- **Pismo Beach** — classic California beach town. ⚠️ **Skip Splash Café** — famous clam chowder is flour-thickened. **Cracked Crab** ✅ is the better pick (whole Dungeness crab, naturally GF, fun and casual).
- **Santa Barbara / Montecito** ⭐ — **Plan dinner here. This is a Celiac destination.** Santa Barbara has 8 dedicated GF facilities in a small city.
  - **Lilac Montecito** ⭐ DEDICATED — 1209 Coast Village Rd, Montecito. **The single best Celiac dining experience on the entire route.** 100% dedicated GF fine dining. California bistro with European technique: buffalo cauliflower, burrata, chicken marsala, fettuccine alfredo, Mediterranean risotto. A Celiac diner reads the full menu and orders anything — an experience most Celiacs almost never get. Chic interior, European patio. Dinner Tues–Sun. **Reserve ahead.**
  - **Lilac Patisserie** ✅ DEDICATED — same team, 100% GF bakery for breakfast/pastries if you're passing through in the morning.

**Camp: Leo Carrillo State Beach** ⭐ — beachfront camping 28 miles north of Santa Monica in Malibu. 135 sites, some directly on the beach. One of the best options near LA for a van.
- Reserve on [reservecalifornia.com](https://www.reservecalifornia.com) — 🟡 Book a few months out

### Day 10 — Apr 15: Los Angeles (Day 1)
**Stay:** Leo Carrillo State Beach (2nd night)

**Best things to do:**
- **Malibu Creek State Park** — beautiful canyon hike from the Malibu area, ~4 miles to the MASH filming location and Rock Pool. Great moderate trail.
- **Santa Monica Pier + Promenade** — classic. Best in the morning before crowds.
- **Venice Beach boardwalk** — unique, colorful, worth a walk
- **Getty Center** — free parking (reserve), world-class art with incredible views over the city and Pacific. Worth 2–3 hrs.

**Celiac dining — Los Angeles:**
- **Shojin** ⭐ DEDICATED — Culver City. **Top pick for LA.** 100% GF vegan Japanese restaurant built specifically for people with Celiac, soy allergy, nut allergy, and multiple intolerances. Sushi, ramen, tempura — all GF, every allergen labeled on the menu. No microwaves, no artificial ingredients. The experience of eating sushi and ramen freely without worrying about soy sauce or tempura batter is something most Celiacs never get. Worth the Culver City drive. (Note: Little Tokyo location is closed — Culver City only.)
- **Malibu Farm** (Malibu Pier) ✅ GF-friendly — California fresh food with ocean views. Good for a casual coastal lunch near camp. Menu labels GF items; staff are knowledgeable.
- **True Food Kitchen** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — Santa Monica (and other LA locations). Clearly marks celiac-safe menu items. Servers trained to distinguish celiac from preference. Reliable for a broader group meal.
- **Erewhon Market** ✅ DEDICATED — multiple LA locations (Venice, West Hollywood, Pacific Palisades). Upscale natural grocer with extensive prepared food, everything clearly GF-labeled. Pricey but completely safe — great for stocking the van with quality GF provisions before the desert crossing.
- ⚠️ **Skip: Bay Cities Italian Deli** (bread-forward, no safe GF option), **Gjusta** (excellent bakery/deli but cross-contamination risk).

### Day 11 — Apr 16: Los Angeles (Day 2) → Begin East
**Camp:** Move inland — **Joshua Tree National Park area** as a transition night before the desert crossing

**Morning LA options:**
- **Griffith Observatory** — drive up for city/ocean views, free entry to grounds, morning is quieter
- **Runyon Canyon** — easy hike above Hollywood, views of the basin
- **The Original Farmers Market** (3rd & Fairfax) — great breakfast/brunch, outdoor stalls. **Celiac note:** **Lotería! Grill** ✅ (corn tortilla-based Mexican) is a reliable pick. Avoid the pastry and bread stalls.
- **Breadblok** ✅ DEDICATED — 1529 Griffith Park Blvd, Silver Lake. 100% GF artisan bakery: organic croissants, sourdough, pastries, gum-free. Good morning stop if you're heading through Silver Lake toward Griffith/Los Feliz. (Santa Monica location closed Oct 2025 — Silver Lake only.)

**Drive to Joshua Tree:** ~2.5 hrs from central LA to the park entrance

**Camp near Joshua Tree:**
- **Jumbo Rocks Campground** (Joshua Tree NP) — 124 sites among giant boulders, first-come-first-served (no reservations). Arrive by noon in April to secure a site. No hookups, no water — your van is ideal for this.
- **Hidden Valley Campground** — smaller, more dramatic boulder setting. Also first-come-first-served.

**Joshua Tree evening:**
- **Cholla Cactus Garden** — short walk through a dense cactus garden, stunning at golden hour
- **Keys View** — sweeping desert panorama including the Salton Sea and San Jacinto Mountains
- **Stargazing** — Joshua Tree is an International Dark Sky Park. One of the best nights of the trip for stars.

**Celiac dining — Joshua Tree / Palm Springs area:**
- **Stock the van in LA before leaving** — Joshua Tree itself has almost nothing reliable.
- **Boozehounds** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — Palm Springs (if routing through or making a detour). Dedicated GF fryer, staff proactively explains cross-contamination precautions. Community-rated as the best Celiac option in the Coachella Valley. Safe dishes: chicken adobo, skirt steak, pork belly, Parmesan truffle fries.
