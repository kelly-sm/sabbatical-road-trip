## Celiac Dining Strategy for This Leg

This route passes through some of the best Celiac-safe dining in the country. A few principles:

- Always say **"Celiac disease"**, not "gluten-free" — it signals medical necessity and changes how kitchens respond.
- **DEDICATED** = 100% gluten-free facility. Safest. **CELIAC-AWARE** = shared kitchen but with dedicated fryers, separate prep, trained staff, and strong celiac community reviews. **GF-FRIENDLY** = menu options only, not vetted for cross-contamination.
- **Stock the van** before entering Big Sur (no reliable options) and before Joshua Tree.
- **Natural co-ops** are reliable fallbacks: Wildberries Marketplace (Arcata), New Leaf Community Market (Santa Cruz), Erewhon (multiple LA locations).

- **Public Coast Brewing Co.** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — 264 3rd St. **This is the standout celiac stop on the Oregon Coast.** The owner's son has Celiac disease, which shapes the whole kitchen culture. Dedicated fryer, gloves changed on request, separate containers. Almost the entire menu available GF: fish and chips, onion rings, burgers with GF buns, clam chowder. Listed in the National Celiac Association restaurant directory. Worth arriving in Cannon Beach hungry.

**Tillamook Creamery:** Hours 10am–6pm. Free self-guided tour. Cheese and ice cream are naturally GF — skip the bakery café items. Big van-friendly parking lot.

**⚠️ Camp correction — Cape Lookout SP is CLOSED April 2026** for park improvements. Beverly Beach is the primary camp.

**Camp: Beverly Beach State Park** (Newport, OR) — 281 sites, trail to a long sandy beach, forest-sheltered. Full hookup and electric/water sites available. Reserve at [oregonstateparks.reserveamerica.com](https://oregonstateparks.reserveamerica.com). Some loops (G/H) are first-come-first-served Apr 1–May 22; book a reservable site in advance.

**Stop notes:**
- **Cape Perpetua:** Free (or America the Beautiful pass). Thor's Well is a dramatic sea hole that fills/drains with waves — more impressive at high tide or just before. Check tide timing.
- **Sea Lion Caves:** ~$16/adult. Open 9am–5pm. **April reality:** Sea lions spend spring and summer on outdoor ledges, not in the cave itself — the dramatic interior cave is the winter draw. Worth stopping if you're curious about the coastal geology; skip if sea lions aren't a priority. Worth calling ahead to ask about current sea lion positions.
- **Oregon Dunes Overlook:** Free pull-off. 5–10 minute walk to a boardwalk with dune-to-ocean views. One of the best quick stops on the Oregon coast.
- **Trees of Mystery:** ~$20/adult. Open 9am–5:30pm. Consistently surprises visitors who expect a tourist trap — the gondola through the redwood canopy and the end-of-trail Native American museum (6 rooms) are genuinely good. The Paul Bunyan statue at the entrance is delightfully kitschy. Worth the stop.
- **Davison Road:** 7.5 miles unpaved, bring cash for the $12 kiosk fee (no cards). Arrive before dark. Your Transit at under 24 ft is within the vehicle limit.

**Celiac dining:**
- **Lunch:** Wildberries Marketplace (Arcata co-op, 811 I St) — deli with labeled GF items, bulk section, good for restocking the van.
- **Alternative dinner before camp:** **Humboldt Bay Bistro** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — 1436 2nd St, Eureka, (707) 443-7339. Bay-front French-Californian with unusual celiac menu transparency. Only if you can arrive by ~7pm and have the appetite after Trees of Mystery.

**Camp: Gold Bluffs Beach Campground (Prairie Creek Redwoods SP)** ⭐ — 26 sites on a black sand beach with elk wandering through camp. Steps from Fern Canyon trailhead. **Reserve ASAP** on [reservecalifornia.com](https://www.reservecalifornia.com) — 🔴 high demand.
- Fallback: **Elk Prairie Campground** in the same park — old-growth redwood setting, elk meadow at dawn/dusk.

**Stop notes:**
- **Griffith Observatory:** Grounds open 5am, building opens noon–10pm on Thursdays. No point waiting for the building on a driving day — the hilltop views over LA are what you want. - **Cholla Cactus Garden:** Stay on the path. The barbed segments detach and embed in skin instantly. Golden hour (around 6:30pm in mid-April) is when the backlit spines glow.
