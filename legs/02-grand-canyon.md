# Leg 2: Grand Canyon
**Joshua Tree → Route 66 → Grand Canyon South Rim → Zion (handoff)**
**Dates:** April 17–19 (2 nights, 2 full days + departure morning)

---

## ⚠️ Key Facts & Watch-Outs

- **No timed entry permit required** at South Rim — just pay the $35 vehicle entrance fee at the gate (no cash accepted; or use an America the Beautiful pass if you have one).
- **Mather Campground** requires advance reservation March–November. **Reserve on recreation.gov — 🔴 do this now.**
- **Desert View Campground** opens April 11, 2026 — will be open for their April 18 stay. Also reserve on recreation.gov.
- **South Kaibab Trail has zero shade** — start by 8am or wait until late afternoon. April temps on the rim are mild (highs ~60–65°F) but the inner canyon gets warm.
- **Hermit Road** is closed to private vehicles spring through fall — free park shuttle runs every 15–30 min from the Village.
- **America the Beautiful Annual Pass** ($80) covers entrance to all national parks. With Grand Canyon + multiple Utah parks ahead, this pays for itself immediately. Buy at the first park entrance gate or online.

**Stop notes:**
- **Needles gas stop:** Gas is cheap in Needles and expensive inside the park — fill up here. The drive from Joshua Tree is ~155 miles of desert; don't assume your tank will cover it all. Last reliable cheap gas before the canyon.
- **Oatman:** US-95 north from Needles, then old Route 66 west over Sitgreaves Pass (elevation 3,550 ft) — a brief but dramatic series of switchbacks above the Colorado River valley. The burros will approach the van; they're used to people but skip feeding them. The Oatman Hotel bar is open daily and worth a 5-minute look — Clark Gable and Carole Lombard honeymooned here in 1939. Free museum in the back. ⚠️ Oatman Hotel restaurant is not Celiac-safe — van snacks or Needles before.
- **Hackberry General Store:** On Route 66, ~30 miles east of Kingman. Open daily approximately 9am–5pm. Not a working store — a pure nostalgia museum with vintage gas pumps, old Corvettes, neon signs, and Route 66 memorabilia packed into the lot. One of the most photographed spots on the entire Mother Road. 20 minutes is enough; it's compact.
- **Williams lunch timing:** Nany's Tacos opens at 11am (closed Tuesdays — Apr 17 is a Friday, open). Arrive by 12:30–1pm to avoid the lunch rush. ⚠️ **Nany's is closed Tuesdays** — not an issue on April 17 (Friday), but flag for future reference.
- **Grand Canyon entrance:** America the Beautiful pass covers the $35 vehicle fee — have it accessible at the window. April weekday afternoons typically have a 10–20 min gate wait; it can spike on weekends. The South Entrance on AZ-64 is the main gate.
- **Mather Point vs Yavapai Point for first view:** Mather Point is 5 minutes from the entrance gate and is the default first look — walk the paved rim path from the parking area. Then move to Yavapai (0.7 miles east along the Rim Trail, or a short drive) for sunset — it faces both east and west, and the geology museum has floor-to-ceiling observation windows you can be inside while the light changes.
- **April sunset:** ~7:32pm at the South Rim. Arrive at Yavapai Point by 7:00pm for the best light. The rim glows orange and red for about 20 minutes before the light drops below the canyon walls.
- **Celiac dinner tonight:** Cook from the van at Mather — this is a long drive day. Or walk to Yavapai Lodge Dining Hall (~10 min from Mather) if you want a sit-down meal without a reservation. Their GF menu is solid and no advance booking needed.

### Recommended Route: The Route 66 Option

Instead of the all-highway I-40 slog, take the Route 66 corridor — it adds ~45 minutes but the stops are genuinely worth it:

**Joshua Tree → I-10 East → US-95 North → Needles, CA**
- Needles is a classic desert crossroads. Gas up here.

**Needles → Oatman, AZ (via old Route 66 over the Black Mountains)**
- The section of Route 66 over Sitgreaves Pass into Oatman is dramatic — steep switchbacks through desert mountains, completely unlike the surrounding flatlands.
- **Oatman** is a well-preserved gold mining ghost town. Wild burros (descendants of miners' pack animals) roam freely through the streets and will walk right up to you. The Oatman Hotel is where Clark Gable and Carole Lombard spent their honeymoon. Free museum inside, bar/restaurant.
- Allow 30–45 min here.

**Oatman → Kingman → Hackberry General Store**
- **Hackberry General Store** — the best single photo stop on Arizona Route 66. An explosion of vintage gas pumps, old cars, neon signs, and Route 66 memorabilia. 30 min east of Kingman on Route 66.
- Great backdrop for a trip photo.

**Hackberry → Williams, AZ**
- **Williams** is the official "Gateway to the Grand Canyon" on Route 66. Charming downtown, good lunch options. 1 hour from the South Rim.
- Grab lunch here. **Celiac dining in Williams:**
  - **Nany's Tacos** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — 324 E Railroad Ave. Best Celiac option in Williams. Corn tortillas throughout, knowledgeable owner, staff understand cross-contamination (not just GF preference). They clean the grill thoroughly for GF orders. Most tacos are GF except two (the red-sauce ones use flour as a thickener — ask). No dedicated fryer so skip chips. Barbacoa tacos are consistently praised. Celiac diners report no reactions.
  - **Red Raven Restaurant** — local favorite for atmosphere but no specific Celiac-safe protocols documented; use caution and ask explicitly about cross-contamination if you go.
  - **Pine Country Restaurant** ✅ GF-FRIENDLY — marks GF items on menu, knowledgeable staff. ⚠️ Hash browns are labeled GF but cooked in a shared fryer — skip fried items.

**Williams → Grand Canyon South Rim**
- ~1 hr north on AZ-64. Arrive mid-to-late afternoon.

## Celiac Dining Strategy for This Leg

Options thin out significantly once you leave the coast. The South Rim is a captive-audience dining situation — know what's safe before you arrive.

- **Stock the van** before leaving LA or Palm Springs. Carry reliable GF provisions through the desert.
- **Say "Celiac disease"** explicitly at every restaurant — especially at Grand Canyon lodge dining where staff turnover is high.
- **El Tovar** is the clear best option on the South Rim — make a reservation.
- **Kanab** is a genuine bright spot between Grand Canyon and Zion — better options than expected for a small town.

## Camping: Two-Campground Strategy

Splitting nights between the two campgrounds gives you the best of both sides of the rim:

### Night 1: Mather Campground
- Largest campground, 1 mile from Canyon Village and trailheads. 327 sites, no hookups.
- Showers and laundry available at the nearby Camper Village (small fee).
- Walk or shuttle to the rim for sunset your first evening.
- 🔴 **Reserve on [recreation.gov](https://www.recreation.gov)** — books up months in advance for April

### Night 2: Desert View Campground
- 26 miles east of the Village on Desert View Drive — smaller (50 sites), quieter, and right at the east rim.
- Open April 11–Oct 11, 2026. No hookups, no showers — your van is ideal.
- Positioned perfectly for departure to Utah: you're already on the east end of the park, just hop on US-89 north toward Zion.
- 🔴 **Reserve on [recreation.gov](https://www.recreation.gov)**

**Stop notes:**
- **Sunrise at Yavapai Point:** Less crowded than Mather Point. Faces east with views up-canyon — you see the far walls glow before the canyon floor lights up. The Yavapai Geology Museum (panoramic observation windows) opens at 8am; worth a look before the hike but the 5-minute walk to the point itself is the priority at sunrise.
- **South Kaibab Trail — April conditions:** April is ideal — rim temps 55–65°F, inner canyon 70–80°F, no snow on the trail by mid-April in a typical year. The trail is entirely on an exposed ridge with zero shade and zero water — bring 2L per person minimum and start before 9am. At Cedar Ridge there are pit toilets, a turnaround sign, and the canyon opens up in all directions. The Ooh Aah Point overlook at 0.9 miles is genuinely spectacular even if you turn around there; it earns its name.
- **Shuttle logistics — South Kaibab:** Parking at the trailhead is extremely limited (a few spots for permit holders). Take the orange Kaibab/Rim Route shuttle from the Visitor Center Plaza or Canyon View Information Plaza — runs every 10–15 min starting about 1 hour before sunrise. Walk to the trailhead from the bus stop is 2 minutes.
- **El Tovar lunch:** The most reliable Celiac-safe full-service meal in the park. Reserve as far ahead as possible at grandcanyonlodges.com — reservations open 6 months out, and April is busy. If you couldn't get a lunch reservation, Yavapai Lodge Dining Hall has no reservation required and has a solid GF menu.
- **Tusayan Ruin & Museum:** Free with park entrance. One of the smallest and best-preserved Ancestral Puebloan ruins in the region, right on Desert View Drive — an easy 20-min stop with a small museum explaining the canyon's human history. Often overlooked by visitors; almost never crowded.
- **Desert View Watchtower:** Open daily 8am–5pm (free with park entrance). Mary Colter's 1932 stone tower built in the style of ancient Puebloan towers. Climb all four interior levels to the top for views extending to the Painted Desert, North Rim, and beyond. The Navajo trading post at the base is genuinely good — not a tourist trap. ⚠️ Watchtower snack bar has limited/risky options for Celiac; rely on van food at this end of the park.
- **Hopi Point sunset:** Stop 7 on the red Hermit Road shuttle (9 stops total). The shuttle runs every 15–30 min and continues until approximately 30 min after sunset — you will not be stranded. Hopi Point juts out into the canyon further than any other rim viewpoint, giving you a 270° panorama. Arrive 45–60 min before sunset for a good position; it gets crowded but is large enough to spread out.
- **Hermit Road shuttle note:** Private vehicles cannot drive Hermit Road spring through fall. The free red shuttle departs from the Village Transfer Area (near Bright Angel Lodge) and stops at 9 viewpoints. Running time end-to-end is about 1 hour each direction.

### Day 13 Celiac Dining on the South Rim:
- **El Tovar Dining Room** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — the best dining option in the park. Has a dedicated GF menu; celiac diners report eating safely here ("my celiac daughter and I ate safely for lunch"). The nicest restaurant on the rim — reserve ahead for dinner. Reservations open 6 months in advance at grandcanyonlodges.com.
- **Yavapai Lodge Dining Hall** ✅ GF-FRIENDLY — has a GF menu; positive reports from celiac travelers (GF bread toasted and handled appropriately). Convenient cafeteria-style, no reservation needed.
- **Bright Angel Restaurant** ⚠️ — GF buns available for burgers, but fries are breaded and the fryer is shared. Order carefully: grilled items only, no fried anything.
- **Desert View Watchtower snack bar** ⚠️ — limited selection, cross-contamination risk. Van snacks are safer here.
- **Strategy:** Eat a real meal at El Tovar (reserve ahead), use Yavapai for convenience meals, cook from the van at Desert View.

**Stop notes:**
- **Desert View sunrise bonus:** If you're awake anyway, the Watchtower at sunrise on a clear April morning is remarkable — the Painted Desert to the east lights up before the canyon does, a completely different color palette from the previous evening's sunset. The Watchtower opens at 8am, but the exterior platform is accessible earlier; walk up to the base and look east.
- **East Entrance departure:** Exiting through the East Entrance puts you directly on US-89 north — no backtracking through the Village. This is exactly why Desert View CG was the right call for Night 2. Stop at the entrance kiosk on the way out if you need a receipt.
- **Echo Cliffs:** The 30-mile stretch of US-89 south of Page, AZ runs along the base of sheer 1,000-foot sandstone cliffs stained in deep reds and oranges. No formal stop needed — they're visible from the road and build anticipation for Utah.
- **Navajo Bridge / Marble Canyon:** Two parallel bridges span Marble Canyon (the upstream beginning of Grand Canyon) 470 feet above the Colorado River. The 1929 historic bridge is now a pedestrian walkway — walk out to the middle for the river view below. California condors (reintroduced and now thriving) frequently perch on the cliff walls nearby; April is a good time to see them. The Navajo Bridge Interpretive Center (open 9am–5pm daily April through October) is free and worth 10 minutes. Total stop: 30–40 min.
- **Vermilion Cliffs:** A few miles north of Navajo Bridge, the cliff face turns from deep red to orange-red as the rock layers change — pull off on the wide shoulder for photos. The condor release site for the reintroduction program is in this area; there's a small parking area and informational sign.
- **Kanab lunch — Celiac dining:** Rocking V Cafe (97 W Center St) opens at 11:30am for lunch. April 19 is a Sunday — call ahead to confirm hours and reserve a table. Wild Thyme Cafe (open ~9am) is a good alternative if you arrive before Rocking V opens. ⚠️ Three Bears was formerly a solid Celiac option but permanently closed in early 2026 — skip.
- **Oscar's Cafe, Springdale:** 948 Zion Park Blvd, (435) 772-3232. Open daily 7am–3pm (breakfast/lunch only). Consistent GF-friendly Mexican: corn tortillas throughout, rice and beans naturally GF, knowledgeable staff. Multiple Celiac traveler reports of safe meals. Great breakfast burrito in a corn tortilla, red chile enchiladas. Arrive before the post-hike rush (~1pm is busy). If you had lunch in Kanab, stop here for a snack or breakfast the next morning.
- **Watchman Campground check-in:** Located at the south entrance to Zion, just inside the park boundary. Check-in at the kiosk — bring your reservation confirmation and America the Beautiful pass. ⚠️ **A/B Loop sites have electric hookups only** — you don't need hookups, but these loops may be slightly more expensive than the non-hookup loops (C/D/E). Your reservation should specify the loop; confirm when booking on recreation.gov that you're in a standard loop if you prefer. Watchman has good cell service, shade trees, and is walking distance to Springdale via the pedestrian bridge.
- **Pa'rus Trail:** The 2-mile paved trail along the Virgin River connects Watchman Campground to the Zion Canyon Visitor Center. Flat, bike-friendly, great for an evening leg-stretcher after a long drive day. Dogs allowed on leash (one of the few Zion trails where they are).
- **April in Zion:** Crowds ramp up significantly starting in mid-April. The Zion Canyon Scenic Drive is closed to private vehicles year-round — you ride the free park shuttle from the Visitor Center (late March through November, roughly every 6–8 min in peak season). Narrows water levels are typically good in April — low enough to wade but the river has character. Rent neoprene booties in Springdale the evening you arrive ($20–30/person).

### Celiac dining in Kanab:
- **Rocking V Cafe** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — 97 W Center St, (435) 644-8001. The best full-service Celiac option in Kanab. New American, menu clearly marks GF items including desserts. Staff trained on Celiac, will do what they can to avoid cross-contamination. Multiple positive Celiac reports. Reserve ahead — it fills up.
- **Wild Thyme Cafe** ✅ CELIAC-AWARE — healthy café with multiple GF options. A celiac diner ate there two nights in a row without issue.
- **Nany's Tacos** (if driving through from Williams earlier and not stopping there) — also has a presence/similar concept.
- ⚠️ **Three Bears** — was a Celiac-friendly option but **permanently closed as of early 2026.** Skip.

## Quick Tips

- **America the Beautiful Pass** — buy before entering Grand Canyon; covers all upcoming Utah parks too.
- **Cell service** is spotty inside the park. Download offline maps (Maps.me or Google offline) before arriving.
- **Free shuttles** run multiple routes through the park during busy season — use them instead of driving between viewpoints.
- **Kanab, UT** is a useful refuel/resupply point between Grand Canyon and Zion. Rocking V Cafe is the best Celiac-safe lunch stop on this stretch — worth building time around.
- **Grand Canyon dining:** Reserve El Tovar as far in advance as possible — it's the only truly reliable Celiac-safe option on the South Rim.
